ARCHIVE: THE BLOOMING STAGE

The Budding Transition

When the bulb on its back grows large, it loses the ability to stand on its hind legs. This weight shift signifies that evolution is near. The bulb begins to release a sweet, pleasant aroma—a precursor to the massive flower it will eventually support.

Olfactory Signaling

As the flower prepares to bloom, the scent intensifies. Other Pokémon often gather around Ivysaur when this happens, attracted by the nutrient-rich pollen. In the wild, this makes Ivysaur a focal point of forest ecosystems.

Sunlight Dependency

Ivysaur spends significantly more time basking in the sun than Bulbasaur. It requires massive amounts of solar energy to fuel the expansion of the flower. If it stops basking, the flower may wilt, causing the Pokémon to grow weak and lethargic.

COMBAT PROTOCOLS

Chlorophyll (Hidden Ability)

In harsh sunlight, Ivysaur’s speed is doubled. This metabolic spike allows it to outpace almost any opponent, making it a dangerous threat in "Sun Teams" when paired with a Torkoal or Groudon.

Specialized Moveset
  • Solar Beam: It gathers light energy in its bud and fires a concentrated beam. Normally takes two turns, but is instant in the sun.
  • Synthesis: Deeply heals itself using solar energy. Most effective at high noon.
Recommended Gear
  • Eviolite: Just like Charmeleon, Ivysaur benefits immensely from this. It turns its balanced 80/80 defenses into a bulky 120/120 spread.
  • Heat Rock: If Ivysaur is the one setting the sun, this item extends the duration of the sunlight from 5 turns to 8.
